# Sidelight controller: quick reference for projectionists

The sidelights (the LED strips along the side walls of the screen) are controlled by a small computer hidden in the equipment above the screen. Five buttons in the booth, on a black box mounted above and behind the desktop PC monitor, let you switch between lighting moods.

---

## The five buttons

| Button | What it does |
| ------ | ------------ |
| **1** | Ambient -- slow, gentle random fades. Good for pre-show or intermission. |
| **2** | Off -- fades everything down to black. Use this to kill the lights. |
| **3** | Heartbeat -- slow double-pulse. Atmospheric. |
| **4** | Sparkle -- pulses up to full brightness. Dramatic. |
| **5** | Knight Rider -- sweeping back-and-forth. Fun. |

**To turn the lights off: press button 2.** It fades them down smoothly over a few seconds.

---

## If something goes wrong during a screening

The lights behave themselves almost all the time. If they start glitching, flickering abnormally, or doing something distracting:

**Step 1: press button 2.** This tells the controller to fade everything off. Wait 5-10 seconds.

**Step 2: if that doesn't work** -- the controller may have crashed. The nuclear option is to cut the power directly.

---

## Nuclear option: cut the power

You need: the long stick from the bar, and a short stepladder.

There are two power strips mounted above the screen, one on each side. Each strip has its own switch or plug. Reach up with the stick and switch off or unplug both strips. The lights will go off immediately.

> **Note:** this cuts power to the controller too. The lights will come back on when power is restored (there's an automatic startup delay of about 30 seconds). If you don't want the lights back on, leave the power off for the duration of the screening.

---

## The controller restarts itself

If the controller crashes, it restarts automatically within a few seconds. If you pressed button 2 before the crash, the lights should come back on in the off/fading state. If not, press button 2 again once the lights reappear.
